Ozimandias

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

I met a traveller from an antique land,
Who said-"Two vast and trunkless legs of stone,
Stand in the desert .... Near them, on the sand,
Half sunk a shattered visage lies, whhose frown,
And wrinkled lip, an sneer of cold command,
Tell that its sculptor well those passions read
Which yet survibe, stamped on these lifeless things,
The hand that mocked them, and the heart that fed;
And on the pedestal, these word appear:
My name is ozymandias, King of Kings;
Look on my works, ye Mighty, and despair!
Nothing beside remains. Round the decay
of that colossal Wreck, boundless and bare
The lone and levelsands stretch far away."
